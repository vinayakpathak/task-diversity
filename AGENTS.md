## Writing style:
- Proofs should be presented in a top-down, modular way.
- First, precisely state the theorem you are going to prove.
- Break down this goal into subgoals and describe how achieving each goal will lead to achieving the goal of proving the theorem.
- For each subgoal, do the same, i.e., state the goal precisely, and then, if it makes sense, break that into subsubgoals, and so on.
- When a subgoal doesn't lend itself into many subgoals, then write down its proof.
- Before writing down any proof, describe the intuition why the proof should work.
- The intuition should be pitched towards a theoretical computer scientist. i.e., assume your audience is a theoretical computer scientist and say whatever they will think is the essence of the proof.
- Try to find proofs that are simple and intuitive.
